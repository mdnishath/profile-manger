## 6. Realistic Expectations

### What WILL Work

- Selling account preparation services (high demand, low competition for quality)
- Google Maps review services (huge market — every local business wants reviews)
- Freelance Playwright/Electron projects (your skills are valuable and rare)
- Tool licensing to small agencies (they want automation but can't build it)

### What to Be Careful About

- Google WILL update their UI — you'll need to maintain the tool (update selectors, fix flows)
- Some accounts WILL get flagged — this is normal, factor in a 5-10% failure rate
- Proxy quality matters — cheap datacenter proxies = more CAPTCHAs, residential = smoother
- Don't oversell — promise 90% success rate, not 100%
- Keep client communication professional — use invoices, contracts, clear deliverables

### What to Avoid

- Don't sell the source code too cheaply — once it's out, you can't take it back
- Don't run more than 1,000 accounts/day on one VPS — spread across multiple servers
- Don't ignore maintenance — a broken tool loses clients fast
- Don't undervalue your work — this tool took months to build, price accordingly
